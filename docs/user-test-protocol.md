# Five-user test protocol

This protocol collects direct feedback without analytics, tracking pixels, accounts, or invented results. Ask permission before recording notes and do not collect names unless necessary.

## Setup

- Test one person at a time with the public judge demo or unpacked extension.
- Give only: “This product turns AI waiting time into English listening practice.”
- Do not coach during the first attempt. Record observable behavior and exact quotes only with consent.
- Target 8–12 minutes per participant.

## Tasks and questions

1. After ten seconds on the first screen, ask: “What do you think this does?” Record whether the core idea was understood.
2. Ask the participant to start the first sprint. Record whether they succeed without help and where they hesitate.
3. Let AI completion interrupt the exercise. Ask what the **AI is ready** screen means and what they would do next.
4. Ask whether they would use this during normal AI work, and why or why not.
5. Show Lecture-to-Sprints and ask them to prepare for an upcoming topic. Ask whether the GPT-5.6-generated pack would be useful.
6. Ask: “What was confusing?”
7. Ask: “Would you prefer automatic start, manual start, or both?”

## Synthesis

After all five sessions, count outcomes only from the table and separate observation from interpretation. Record the top three repeated issues, severity, proposed change, and whether it was implemented. Do not convert five qualitative sessions into broad adoption or performance claims.
